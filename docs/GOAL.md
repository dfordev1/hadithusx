# Product goal

## Mission

Build an open, evidence-first hadith data standard and scholarly workbench: a hadith equivalent of the durable interchange formats and research environments that transformed biblical textual study, while respecting the distinct methods of Islamic scholarship.

The system should let researchers preserve, compare, review, cite, and exchange:

- source witnesses and exact provenance;
- matn text and textual variants;
- isnad chains, branches, and transmission expressions;
- narrator mentions and carefully reviewed person identities;
- books, chapters, parallel narrations, classifications, and scholarly judgments;
- competing claims without silently turning one opinion into universal truth.

## Product principles

1. Evidence is preserved before interpretation.
2. A mention in a source is separate from a historical person identity.
3. Machine suggestions never become scholarly facts automatically.
4. Every important assertion has provenance, review state, and an accountable author or process.
5. The interchange format is useful without the web application.
6. Builds and imports are deterministic and independently verifiable.
7. Software readiness and scholarly verification are reported separately.
8. The architecture must eventually support the whole hadith corpus, not only the intentions pilot family.

## Definition of success

The project succeeds when independent institutions can encode their own collections, validate them, exchange them without losing evidence, compare parallel narrations, build reproducible isnad networks, and conduct review without depending on one private database or one scholarly opinion.
